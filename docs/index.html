Key Points

  • $3.6 trillion annually on health, less than 3 percent of that spending is directed toward public health and prevention.

  • Public health spending as a proportion of total health spending has been decreasing since 2000 and falling in inflation-adjusted terms since the Great Recession.

  • U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) is the primary driver of federal public health funding through its grant programs to the states and large cities.

  • Most states (39) and the District of Columbia increased or maintained funding for public health budgets in FY 2019, but 11 states decreased such funding.

Technical

The heatmap and correlogram were generated via the heatmaply package in R. From the vignette, a heatmap is a popular graphical method for visualizing high-dimensional data, in which a table of numbers are encoded as a grid of colored cells. The rows and columns of the matrix are ordered to highlight patterns and are often accompanied by dendrograms. Heatmaps are used in many fields for visualizing observations, correlations, missing values patterns, and more.
